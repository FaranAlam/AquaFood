Introduction

  • 🌍 Rising concern of food and water contamination worldwide
  • ⚠️ Millions suffer annually due to adulterated or unsafe consumption
  • 🔬 Traditional testing methods are slow, expensive, and lab-based
  • ❌ Lack of affordable, real-time, consumer-level detection devices
  • ✅ Proposed Solution: Hybrid Non-Contact Detector using multi-sensor fusion ( UV, Gas, Color, and Temperature sensors) for real-time quality analysis

Design Methodology (Part 1)

  • 1. Requirement & Specification Gathering: Define target contaminants (microbial, chemical, adulterants), detection limits, portability, budget, and regulatory constraints
  • 2. System Architecture & Component Selection: Choose hybrid sensors (UV, Gas, Color, Temperature) and microcontroller (ESP32 / Raspberry Pi). Design a modular, non-contact optical enclosure
  • 3. Hardware Prototyping: Build the prototype integrating sensors, optics, power, and wireless connectivity (Wi-Fi / Bluetooth). Conduct calibration and initial validation

Design Methodology (Part 2)

  • 4. Data Acquisition & Calibration: Acquire raw sensor signals from clean and contaminated samples. Calibrate results against laboratory references
  • 5. Signal Processing & Feature Extraction: Denoise data using filters, apply FFT or spectrogram transformations, and compute biospeckle or optical statistics
  • 6. Machine Learning Model Development: Train classifiers (SVM, RandomForest, CNN) and regression models for impurity estimation. Perform cross-validation and hyperparameter optimization

Design Methodology (Part 3)

  • 7. Integration & UI: Deploy the trained ML inference model on an edge device. Develop an interactive dashboard (Streamlit, Gradio, or web) for real-time visualization and data logging
  • 8. Validation & Field Testing: Conduct testing on blind food and water samples. Compare system outputs with laboratory reference results. Evaluate performance metrics
  • 9. Optimization & Documentation: Optimize sensor arrangement, sampling geometry, and power usage. Prepare user manual, SOPs, and final project report
